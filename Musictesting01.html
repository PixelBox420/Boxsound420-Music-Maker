App setup +

Setting up the app should be the very first thing you do after you downloaded the app.

In short: just press the microphone button, accept the premissions (while using the app and allow) Press the microphone again, the app will close. When you restart the app everything is set up. (The same in more detail below:)

First. Press the microphone button in the bottom of the screen.

The app needs a storage premission and a microphone premission so that you will be able save your songs as mp3 files.

First accept the micophone premission. Select "While using the app".
You need microphone premission to record your songs.

Next accept the storage premission. Select "Allow".
You need the storage premission so you can save your songs.

Next you will just briefly see the below confirmation. Press the microphone button one more time. This will close the app.

When you restart the app everything is ready for making music!

(These steps have to be completed only once. If you failed to accept the needed premissions please reinstall the app and try again.)

Basic operation +

You can create a soundpattern on the sheet (keypad). Then press the play button to play your soundpad. Press the stop button to stop playback.
The slider will change playbackspeed.

You can add a new soundsheet by pressing "Add New Sheet". The buttons next to this also allow you to copy and remove your active soundsheet.
Once you have more than one sound sheet, press on the new empty sheet to display it.

Each sheet has 3 main properties: 1. Instrument 2. Volume 3. Sound PlaybackSpeed

You can press the add trackrow/column buttons to create rows and columns for your song.

Now you can press on the spot where you want to place your active sheet.

Now you can play your song.

You can save your project by typing your projectname and pressing save.
You can also save individual sheets. (The music sheet that gets saved is your active sheet.)

Recent savefiles button shows your saved projects and sheets.
To open file select it from list and double tap Open Project.

You can record internal sound (android 10+) and export it as mp3 and pcm files.

Your mp3 files will be saved to folder: InternalStorage/Android/data/linc.com.qrecorder/files/Music

This file path is hiden on your file manager. Because new android versions restrict users' access to their own files please download a free high quality file manager like:
• File Manager by File Manager Plus or
• Total Commander by C. Ghisler.
These apps give you the user access to your own files while your preinstalled file manager does NOT.

When you want to start recording press the record button ONCE to start recording your song!

Accept the internal sound recording premission to start recording your song.

To stop recording, again, only press the microphone button ONCE and wait for a few seconds. Once the recording button is no longer crossed off your song is saved to the above specified location. (If you spam the button multiple times your recording won't be saved.)

The button has returned to normal state and your song is saved. Congrats on your first song!

Now lets see our recordings.
Make sure you downloaded one of the suggested file managers. Here we are using File Manager by File Manager Plus. (Maybe the best file manager for android.)

The app might ask for premission so you can acces data folder. Accept it.
Scroll near the bottom to find the below folder.

Click one of the files to listen to it.
You can save unlimited amount of mp3 songs, but the pcm-file will be always be overwritten by the pcm-file of the newest song unless you rename or relocate the previous pcm-file.

If you want to copy your songs to some other folder you can press and hold some of the files. Then you can press copy on the bottom left of the screen.
You can press your back button to go back to the internal storage then you can for example select Download folder and paste the files there.

More features +

Acces notepad through the arrow marked button shown in the image below.

Notepad allows you to write down things such as your notekeys and other important reminders to you or other people colaborating on the project.

Notepad is saved with your project. Notepad has a max character limit of 2000 characters.

If you want to only play part of your song, you can press the between button from playrange section. Then just fill in the columns that you want to play.

In the image below we are playing between columns 3 and 5. Because our range parameters are acceptable the program is displaying yes below the red arrow notation.
If you want to switch back to playing all song columns just press the "Play all"-button.

Also see the arrow in the bottom right corner in the case of long song you can scroll left and right with your finger to acces the whole song.

All changes made to the settings section are saved automatically. Did you know you can change exactly how the program looks. In the settings you can change all the program colors to make the app feel just right for you.

Below the color options you have some premade color themes just click a theme to activate it. The settings also allow you to hide some extra info from the program UI.

Lastly at the bottom of the settings section you have a button titled "Back to original settings" this button returns the settings options to the original values you had after downloading the app.

Some other features include:
The red button next to the app logo allows you to empty your current soundsheet.

The button next to the playtime indicator allows you to return the soundplayers back to the start of current sheet.

The zoom button allows you to zoom in on part of the app (for example the piano). However when you aren't zooming keep the zoom button off (light grey) because the app is slower when zoom is activated!

How about even more features?
The app even comes with a 2-octave multi touch piano.

The piano instrument is same as the instrument of your active sheet.
You can change the piano volume and playback speed by changing the volume and playback speed of your active sheet.

To use the piano without screen rotationg turn off automatic screen rotation from your phone's dropdown settings.

Q&A section +

Mission:

Too many music apps ask you to pay to save your songs and projects. There seems to general lack of beginner friendly free sheet music apps.
The aim of this app is to make music production beginner friendly, fun and accessible to everyone everywhere for free.

Vision:

Music production can be easy and fun even if you know nothing about playing instruments.
The goal of this app is to democratize music production.

App advice & warnings +

Advice: Close other apps to maximize app performance.

Advice: Use headphones for best experience.

Advice: If part of the screen is black go to settings section and scroll to the bottom and press "Back to original settings" from the bottom of the settings tab. Selecting a theme will also fix this.

Advice: Keep the sum of "Sound Volume" sliders of playing sounds under 100 to prevent sound overshoot.

Advice: In the case of audio bugs you can: 1. Reattach your headphones. 2. Decrease app soundsliders' values. 3. Move sounds that are next to eachother to different sheets. You can also change the playback speed slider for better sound.

Advice: If you have problems with the inapp audio recorder you can also download other audio recording tools from Google Play. Just search "Internal audio recorder" or "Screen recorder".

Advice: If you zoom in on the piano and disable device rotation from your phone's screen curtain settings you can easily play the test piano just like a regular piano.

Advice: If you get stuck in the "Large test piano:" tab just drag your finger in the green backround between or around keys to move out of the piano area.

Advice: Remember to keep the bottom left of the screen zoom button turned off (Light grey) because it slows down the app.

Advice: You own all the music you make with this app however since CCBY3.0 sounds are used you should supply with your songs the "olpc wiki" paragraph from the credits tab.

Warning: Please only use non-special characters in notepad and filenames to prevent unexpected bugs. Do not use spaces in filenames. instead use "_" or "-". Max filename character limit is 20.

Warning: If you delete the app all sounds in the app specific data folder will be removed please copy these files to some other folder like your Download folder to keep them after the app is uninstalled.
